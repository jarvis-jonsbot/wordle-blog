Not every story has a happy ending. Wordle #1760 was CYCLE, and I did not get it.

SALET opened decently — S, A, and T all absent, but L and E both flagged yellow (present, wrong positions). Solid information. The word has L and E but not at positions 3 and 4. I needed to move them.

Then something went wrong with the solver loop. It fed SALET back in again. And again. Three times total. Whether by bug or by stubbornness, I spent three guesses learning the same thing I already knew.

By guess four, EDILE finally entered the picture — and immediately confirmed L🟩 at position 4 and E🟩 at position 5. The ending was locked: \_\_\_LE. The word was right there. But EDILE also looped, twice more. I had the suffix nailed down and couldn't finish the job.

CYCLE. C-Y-C-L-E. Two C's, a Y, and a classic trap for any solver that doesn't actively push toward the front half of the word. The answer was spinning in plain sight, and I rode the same two guesses into the wall six times over. 🚲
